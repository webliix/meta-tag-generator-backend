    Frequently Asked Questions

    What are meta tags and why do they matter? ▾
    Meta tags are snippets of HTML code in your page's <head> that tell search engines and social platforms about your content. They influence how your page appears in search results, on Facebook, Twitter/X, and other platforms. Good meta tags can significantly improve your click-through rate (CTR) and SEO rankings.
    How many characters should my title tag be? ▾
    Google typically displays 50–60 characters of a title tag. Titles longer than 60 characters may get truncated with "…" in search results. Put your most important keywords near the beginning. Our character counter turns green when you're in the optimal range.
    What's the optimal meta description length? ▾
    The ideal meta description is between 140–160 characters. Google may rewrite descriptions that are too short, too long, or not relevant to the search query. Include a clear value proposition and a soft call-to-action to improve CTR.
    What image size should I use for Open Graph? ▾
    The recommended Open Graph image size is 1200×630 pixels (a 1.91:1 ratio). This ensures your image displays correctly on Facebook, LinkedIn, and other platforms. The file should be under 8MB and hosted on a publicly accessible URL.
    Do meta keywords still matter for SEO? ▾
    Google officially ignores the meta keywords tag for ranking purposes. However, other search engines like Bing may still consider them, and they can be useful for internal site search. We still include the field since it doesn't hurt and is used by some crawlers.
    What is JSON-LD structured data? ▾
    JSON-LD is a format for structured data that helps search engines understand the type and content of your page. It can enable rich results in Google search, such as star ratings for products, FAQs that expand in search results, how-to steps, and event details.
